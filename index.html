Why me

Six things I bring to a classroom that most AI instructors don't.

Reality-tested, not hype-tested

I teach what breaks AI projects — and how to design around it. Not "AI is transforming everything," but the actual friction points: data quality, process fit, user trust.

Enterprise + GTM credibility

I scaled an enterprise platform to $100M+ ARR by navigating complex buying cycles: Legal, Finance, IT, and end users all at once. That maps directly to AI adoption.

Hands-on technical enough for labs

I can run Python notebooks, build practical assignments, and demonstrate prompt engineering, RAG concepts, and evaluation — not just wave at them on a slide.

Business ↔ engineering translator

Most students need to operate between teams, not inside one. I teach how to frame use cases, define success metrics, and communicate requirements to technical teams.

Portfolio-driven course design

Students finish with a real deliverable — a use-case brief, evaluation plan, workflow prototype, or governance checklist. Something they can show an employer, not just a grade.

Tool literacy + judgment

Students learn when not to use GenAI, how to spot hallucinations, how to evaluate outputs, and how to design escalation paths and guardrails.
